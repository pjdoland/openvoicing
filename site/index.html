Open-source living sheet music

Sheet music that plays, slows down, and syncs to real recordings.

OpenVoicing turns a score into something you can practice with and share — built on an open file format you own, not a service you rent.

An open format, not a hosted service

Most interactive-notation tools keep your music on their servers. OpenVoicing does the opposite: every piece is a self-contained .ovb file holding the score, its recordings, and the sync map. You own it, you can host it anywhere, and the player is just static files. No account, no lock-in.

01

Practice, not just playback

Slow a passage down without changing pitch, loop it, add a count-in, and follow a metronome. The tools you actually reach for when learning a piece.

02

Sync to real recordings

Line a real performance up with the score, bar by bar, so playback of the audio follows the notation and you can loop and slow the real thing.

03

A real editor

Import MusicXML or Guitar Pro and edit the notes, voices, ornaments, dynamics, and lyrics. Autosaves as you go; export MusicXML or a bundle.

04

Yours to keep

Export a .ovb bundle and it's a single portable file. Share it, archive it, or embed it — it plays the same everywhere, forever.

Put any piece on your own site

Host a bundle anywhere static, drop in the script, and point a div at it. That's the whole integration — the same player you see above.

<script src="https://your-host/openvoicing-embed.js"></script>
<div data-openvoicing-bundle="https://example.com/tune.ovb"></div>
